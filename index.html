Therapist

Therapists are an important aspect in having a happy and fulfilling life. Their job is to listen to your problems and concerns in order to either advise you or just hear you out. Therapists usually work in offices or clinics, their work environments are usually laid back and relaxed in order to help put their patients at ease.

In order to become a Therapist, one would have 2 options. Option A would require 3 years of supervised counseling experience, while Option B would require 2 years of supervised counseling experience alongside an additional 30-semester credit counseling. On top of that, both options require the standard 60-credit master's degree + bachelor's degree. Ways to obtain licensure change from state to state, but in New Jersey one needs to acquire a LPC, LCSW, or LMFT. Popular schools that provide good Therapy programs are University of Florida, NYU, and Northwestern University

Skills usually considered necessary in order to pursue a successful career in therapy include open-mindedness, empathy, patience, and observation. Now, and even in the near future, Therapy will be a field that provides a lot of opportunity because in recent years mental health has become more accepted and known

Therapist Salary in New Jersey. $52,800 is the 25th percentile. Salaries below this are outliers. $73,100 is the 75th percentile.
